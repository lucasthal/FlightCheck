About FlightCheck

FlightCheck is a checklist and quick-reference app for student and private pilots, covering common training and personal aircraft including the Cessna 152, 172 (M/N/P/S), 182, Piper Warrior and Archer, Beechcraft Baron and Bonanza, Mooney M20, Cirrus SR22, and more.

Each aircraft profile includes phase-by-phase normal procedures, emergency and abnormal procedures, V-speeds, performance tables (takeoff, landing, climb, cruise) transcribed from POH Section 5, and maneuver procedures with ACS tolerances.

Common questions

Where does the V-speed and performance data come from?

All V-speeds, operating limitations, and performance tables are transcribed from official Pilot's Operating Handbooks (POHs) for each specific aircraft variant. Source references (POH section / figure number) are included in the relevant table notes.

Does FlightCheck work offline?

Yes. Once an aircraft profile is loaded, checklists and reference data are available without an internet connection. Sign-in is optional and only used to sync preferences across devices.

How do I report wrong data?

If you spot an error in any V-speed, table value, or procedure, please email or open a GitHub issue with the aircraft model, the page in the app, and the POH section that disagrees. Aviation data corrections are prioritized.

What about my privacy?

FlightCheck collects only the minimum needed to run the app (email for sign-in and preferences for cross-device sync). No advertising, no analytics tracking, no third-party data sharing. Full details on the Privacy Policy page.

For reference and study only. FlightCheck data is sourced from official Pilot's Operating Handbooks but is provided for reference and study only. Always verify all procedures, V-speeds, and performance figures against the actual POH for your specific aircraft and serial number before flight. FlightCheck is not a substitute for the POH/AFM, FAA regulations, or your CFI's guidance.
